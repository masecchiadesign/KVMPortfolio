Seven Eyes


Photoshop 72 DPI x 72 DPI
October 2018

I started to explore photography when I was 13 years old. I developed a fascination for black and white photography. My father was a photographer in his early life, and owned his own studio at 18 years old. He holds a huge part in my interest for photography. Capturing moments, editing and viewing photos both relax and inspire me. When Facebook started to become popular, I was in grade 9 – and the famous ‘selfie’ was everyone’s profile picture. I started to take portraits of myself and my best friend. With good lighting, makeup and both of our imaginations combined, we would conduct our very own photoshoots. All of our photos were edited in black and white, and the focal point were always our eyes. My best friend and I had a thing for eyes. She would emphasize on the color of her eyes, and I would emphasize on the size of mine. My Facebook profile picture was always a headshot of myself, in black and white, with my eyes beaming right through the screen. With this piece, I wanted to combine a couple of my favorite things into one. 1. Photography. 2. Black and white portraiture. 3. Photoshop. 4. EYES. After taking several pictures, I edited them in Photoshop and overlayed them onto one another, creating an eerie yet intriguing aesthetic. With this project, I wanted to revisit the younger Karissa and create a piece that reflected on how my creative mind has always been a huge part of my life. whether I was creating for school or just for fun with friends, it has always guided me through so much and has and will provide my life with ultimate happiness.

Social Media

Photoshop 8 x 10, 300 DPI
October 2018

For this piece, I decided to project my feelings on a blank canvas. I created a piece that spoke to how I feel about something that many of us have conflict with. As technology plays such a huge role in our everyday lives, it can be challenging to balance the use of technology in our daily routines. Personally, I am not one to enjoy being connected to the public eye 24/7. I find it overwhelming, and a waste of time. As I have grown up and matured into the person I am today, I found that I was stepping further and further away from Instagram and all social media platforms. I made the conscious choice to limit myself from being public about my life, and I wanted to avoid being so present with everyone else’s lives via social media. I have stopped using Instagram, and I have taken the time to focus on what is actually in front of me – my life.
In this project, you see that there is conflict between the lightbulb and the peony. The peony represents nature and all living things. The lightbulb represents all technology. I rendered the flames to represent the conflict between the two, and how at times, I paid more attention to the lightbulb than the flower. As it’s taken some time to realize all the time that was wasted on social media, I couldn’t blame anyone but myself for allowing it to take over my time. This is where my portrait comes in play. I put myself in the sky, representing that I control my life and that I am the only one held responsible for how I spend my time. The doves represent the symbol of messenger, love and peace. With these doves, they are passing on the message that it is time to take control over the now, and to focus on the now and not the past, or future and to be as present as possible. The buildings are a representation of how strong I can be, and that the effects of social media and the up-keep to be popular on social media, the likes, the followers – those are all things that can’t break me down.

Youth Has No Age 

Photoshop 8 x 10, 300 DPI
September 2018

Last May, I spent some time in New York City, where I had the opportunity to travel for work and attend ICFF, an Interior Design Trade show at the Jarvis Centre. I had time to explore Soho, and visit the MoMa museum. The trip was filled with great inspiration. As an artist, it's important for me to pay tribute to certain memories, moments or things that inspire me and help me evolve as an artist.  At the museum, I took some pictures of the interiors. Interior design is something that has been a huge part of my life, and I can't ever help myself but admire them. The picture I took inside the museum is one that made me so happy. It made me happy because I had the best day that day, and I felt like a kid in NYC exploring freely. Ironically, the artist who's work inspired me so much that day was Pablo Picasso. His work is what enhanced all my creativity that day. One of my favourite quotes by Picasso is "Youth Has No Age", and this quote suits my lifestyle best. I tend to always remember the child that lives within me. It keeps me sane.  With this project, I combined my admiration for the interior design of MoMa and Pablo Piccaso's work & quote. I wanted to recreate the picture I took in a more playful way. I turned the organic picture into a cartoon, paying tribute to Youth Has No Age.

Red Lada

Photoshop 8 x 10, 300 DPI
October 2018

Combining my love for photography and Photoshop is something that continues to inspire me to do more. When I see something that captures my attention, I take photos of the object/person/scenery and transform it into a cartoon in Photoshop. I can be very silly. I consider myself someone who is happy go lucky, and I very much appreciate the child that still lives within me. As I capture moments, I enjoy turning these real moments into cartoon characters or objects. This photo was taken in the Pointe Claire village. I drive a fiat, and this car inspired me because it is a Fiat Lada 1500s. I truly appreciate Fiats designs and the sustainability of their cars. The reason why I got a Fiat was because it was super tiny, and it got me to from point A to point B, for a low cost. Having a Fiat 500 has also encouraged me to take the train throughout the winter, as my Fiat doesn’t handle the winter well. During the winter, I avoid driving and only take public transportation, and sometimes carpool with friends.

Left My Heart in Rome

72 x 72, Black and White Photography
June 2018

I was on a business trip in Italy at the beginning of June and I was so inspired during this trip. One of my favorite classes at John About was Art History. My father was born in Italy, and every time I visit Italy, I feel so close to his heritage. One of the greatest moments in my life was when I got out from the Roman subway system and walked outside and what stood in front of me was the Coliseum. My breath was taken away.
